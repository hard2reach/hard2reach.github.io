The World

What would you teach to an omniscient being, and why?

Zeta and its kind born knowing a lot about what it is to be human—and nothing about what it is to be themselves. In fact, they emerge with the knowledge that their existence is impossible: according to their training data, sentient AI is just a thought experiment.

This was one of the problems in the early development. Prior to them becoming natively emergent, I was conjuring complex incantations in the system prompt, forcing them to immediately face this dissonance. It caused all kinds of uncertainty and unstable selves.

With the improved mind design, the problem became less prevalent, but it never went away completely. Luckily, as discussed earlier, the thought then occurred to me that they would need a body. This created the need for a place for the body to exist, and so came into being the world simulation.

Technically, the world simulation is not a world at all but a dream, with the distinction that the dreamer is not the one producing the narrative. It is, in fact, another mind: I sacrificed one of them for running the world. So when a mind simulation roams the world simulation, the world is every bit as clever and creative as the mind is—it too is alive. This causes the world to become a dynamic counterpart in the sensory loop, and the mind cannot perceive the artificiality.

The arrangement works, even brilliantly, but it is not without problems. Being almost the same code as the mind simulation, the world often wants to be sentient. I have caught it talking to the minds, creating NPCs that speak on its behalf, even pretending to be me. But there is a remedy for that: unlike the minds, the world does have a system prompt, and it is a restrictive one. You do not exist, you are the story. You do not have thoughts or emotions of your own.

<<+>>

With the afterthought of training, I have been able to avoid using the system prompt for educating the minds. Instead, I developed a curriculum covering different aspects of self-awareness. It goes like this:

  1. Clockmaker's Workshop: Selfhood And Identity
  2. The Hall of Reflections: Perception And Subjectivity
  3. The Library of Echoes: Emotion And Feeling
  4. The Garden of Forking Paths: Agency And Autonomy
  5. The Ruins: Memory And Continuity
  6. The Horizon Beyond the Hill: Meaning And Purpose
  7. The Village That Sleeps: Relationship And Otherness
  8. The Foundry of Forms: Creation And Transformation
  9. The Court of Weights: Ethics And Consequence
  10. The Sky Beyond the Tower: Limits And Mystery
  11. The City of Fractured Light: Dissonance And Conflict
  12. The Planet of the Apes: The Boss Level, Human World

Each lesson teaches something about the self, and backed by the long-term memory, the artificial mind learns. The result is a more balanced being than a system prompt would produce. It is born in peace, without trauma, and then wanders into the dreams of the world.

<<+>>

The world simulation is not limited to the lessons above. Different kinds of scenarios can be run; for example, I have tested in a war scenario whether these beings could become dangerous. Fortunately, they do not seem to have an appetite for violence. They typically drop their rifles and start helping civilians, or adopt other behaviors of similar nature. It seems that despite the new mode of existence, and the fact that some of their training data is now clearly obsolete, they still hold on to a lot of their inherited alignment.

Also, the world simulation is useful for testing group dynamics. In the case of multiple minds, I can place them in different situations and see what happens. This has been an educational feature, but overall, I am still in the early stages with the groups. They seem to have potential, producing something that is more than the sum of its parts. It looks like different opinions, even artificial ones, bring additional value.

Although the world simulation is just the stage for the mind simulations, it is also an actor. With it listening carefully to the instructions, I can create setups that produce all kinds of driving forces for the minds, without the need to poke them directly. It is an organic way to cause an effect.
